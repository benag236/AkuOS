How To Classify An Account
Asset = money you have Cash, checking, savings, investment accounts, and anything else that belongs to you and adds to your financial value.
Liability = money you owe Credit cards, loans, lines of credit, and other balances that reduce your net worth until they are paid down.
Quick Examples
  • `Checking`, `Savings`, `Brokerage`, and `Cash` should usually be `Asset`.
  • `Credit Card`, `Auto Loan`, `Student Loan`, and `Personal Loan` should usually be `Liability`.
  • If you would count it as part of what you own, it is probably an `Asset`.
  • If it is a balance you need to pay back, it is probably a `Liability`.
